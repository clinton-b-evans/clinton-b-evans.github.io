Roles of Value, Empathy & Self-Awareness

All our decisions, choices, opinions come from our belief system and our values. Do you believe something is possible, do you believe in your own abilities? These all help guide and direction us in life. When it comes to learning it is very important to be comfortable not knowing something. The Hierarchy of Competence shows us that at the very base, we have unconscious incompetence, meaning we don't know that we don't know something. Being self-ware helps us accept this and then work towards conscious incompetence (knowing that we don't know).
Sometimes we also need to have empathy for our self as well as others.
Empathy for others helps foster betting working relationships and much better understanding. They say: "Always be kind to those you meet because you have no idea the demons they may be fighting". Something like that. To answer the original question a little bit more directly:
Values, Empathy and Self-Awareness are all tools in our emotion intelligence toolkit that we can use to console.log() out our thoughts, while inspecting others (with kindness) all so that we can return() better outcomes... both in personal and professional areas of our lives.

We Core, Therefore We Are (Why EDA Cores)

EDA cores because the mission is to create job-ready developers. This means having the skillset of technical as well as emotional intelligence. I think Core helps people make better decisions. Me, for example, I was just about to finish (yes I started typing it for ages) a story about someone I dated who could not take feedback and the story was great... it has drama, comedy, a lesson... Aesop would be taking notes... BUT, using my newly refreshed core skills, I did some reflecting and it's really not fair or nice to use someone (even removing their names) in a story like that. Don't you just love seeing new teachings happening right in front of you? Amazing.

To Core, Or Not To Core

The Core has been a great use of time! I don't know if you know this BUT by having Core and Tech parts, gives students options to bounce between the left and right brain working during the week.
It's genius... but you already knew that... EDA Senpais.... we see you... :D
On a serious note, Core Core Core some more. I think this was an absolute must. It has helped alot of students that are heavy on the tech, have to reach inside themselves and dig a little deeper.
I feel the time on Core was a must. We can buy courses, books, watch YouTube videos to keep the coding edge sharp. Having guided Core work that we could not find so easily is like striking gold on your first go. So inclosing.... I would like to justify my answer on why I think Core is not a waste of time, but the EDA core curriculum justifies itself.
